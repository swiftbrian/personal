Strategic Vision — 2026–2030

The Agentic
Inflection Point

AI agents are reshaping how the physical world operates. SafetyCulture sits at the exact intersection where this transformation will be most disruptive — and most valuable.

01

The Threats

Platform
Bypass
As AI agents become capable of autonomous task execution, enterprises will ask: "Why do workers fill out checklists when an agent can observe, verify, and act directly?" Microsoft, ServiceNow, and Salesforce are building operations agents that don't need SafetyCulture's templates — they want the data, which they'll try to capture themselves. The risk isn't disruption. It's irrelevance.
Critical
Template Commoditization
LLMs can generate industry-specific checklists in seconds. The 10,000-template library — once a moat — is becoming a commodity. The templates aren't the moat. The network, context, and decade of execution history are.
Critical
Agent-Native Competitors
Well-funded startups unburdened by legacy architecture are building operations platforms designed from the ground up for a world where the primary user is an agent, not a human. The window to pre-empt them is now.
High
ERP Absorption
SAP, Oracle, and vertical incumbents (Procore, Toast) are adding AI-native safety modules. With existing integrations and CFO relationships, they threaten SafetyCulture's foothold in core verticals through consolidation pressure.
High
Passive Observation Shift
Computer vision and IoT sensors enable continuous, passive safety monitoring — without a human conducting inspections. The scheduled inspection workflow at SafetyCulture's core faces structural challenge as "observe, don't report" becomes viable.
High
Tech Debt Velocity Gap
Older architecture means AI-native features take longer to ship. Well-funded startups with clean stacks iterate faster. If agent features feel bolted-on, customers notice. Innovation velocity is now a survival metric.
Medium
Liability Ambiguity
When an AI agent signs off on a safety inspection and an incident occurs, who is liable? Regulators are unprepared. Moving too fast creates legal exposure; moving too slow cedes ground. SafetyCulture must help define the standard.

One number that answers: "How do we improve today?"

The Five Star Rating is SafetyCulture's most powerful strategic primitive — a single, universal, comparable measure of operational health that every leader from site supervisor to CEO understands instantly. It spans safety, quality, compliance, training, and asset health. Not just a safety metric.

Every low score is a prescription, not just a diagnosis. Because it decomposes into scored dimensions, an agent generates a ranked improvement plan automatically — "fix these three things and your score moves from 3.8 to 4.3 within 30 days."

At scale, it becomes something unprecedented: the first universal benchmark for operational excellence. A Brisbane logistics company can compare against Rotterdam. An insurer can underwrite based on live scores, not annual audits. SafetyCulture becomes infrastructure for how the world transacts on operational trust.

Site Level
Live site scoring
Every site gets a live rating, updated as inspections complete and issues close. A critical item unresolved drops the score in real-time — and the agent tells the manager exactly why and what to fix first.
Portfolio Level
Executive portfolio view
34 hotels, 12 construction sites, 200 warehouses — ranked by rating, live. The outlier surfaces immediately. The agent explains why and generates an intervention plan before the COO asks for one.
Industry Benchmark

Compare against anonymized industry peers. Top-quartile performers are identified. The three practices they share are surfaced. This benchmark data is SafetyCulture's most defensible moat — deepening with every new customer.
Agent Trigger

A site drops below 3.5? The Site Agent automatically schedules an emergency inspection, reassigns overdue tasks, and escalates to the area manager. The Five Star Rating is the heartbeat that drives autonomous action.
Supply Chain

Enterprise customers begin requiring Five Star Ratings from suppliers as a condition of procurement. SafetyCulture becomes the infrastructure for how the world transacts on operational trust — not just a compliance tool.
Insurance

Insurers use live Five Star Ratings to adjust premiums dynamically. This is a fundamentally new revenue model — and an entirely new reason for customers to stay on the platform and improve their score.

Tech Stack — The Build Strategy

The older tech stack is a real constraint — but also a forcing function for good architecture. The approach is the Strangler Fig pattern: named after the tree that grows around an existing trunk and gradually replaces it, it means building the new system incrementally around the old one — new customers get the modern experience, existing customers stay stable, and the legacy surfaces are retired piece by piece over time. No risky big-bang rewrite. No disruption to the existing business.

The Challenge
Agent integration complexityLLM/agent capabilities require real-time data access, streaming, and event-driven triggers — difficult to retrofit into older service architectures.
Data model rigidityAgents need fluid, context-rich access across entities. Legacy schemas designed for form capture don't expose the relationship graph agents need to reason across.
Migration risk76,000 organizations depend on current workflows. Any architectural change must maintain backwards compatibility — can't break what's working.
Engineering talentAI-native engineers want to work on cutting-edge stacks. Older architecture creates headwinds for attracting the team needed to build the future.
The Strategy
Agent Layer as a new serviceBuild the agentic platform as a separate, modern microservice — event-driven, streaming-native, LLM-ready — sitting above the existing platform and reading from its APIs. New capabilities ship fast without touching legacy code.
Operational Knowledge GraphA new service that indexes SafetyCulture's data in a format optimized for LLM context and agent reasoning. The brain layer — separate from the transactional layer.
Strangler Fig migrationNew product surfaces (agent UI, co-pilot, Five Star Rating) built entirely modern. Legacy surfaces remain stable. Over 3 years, legacy surfaces are deprecated as new ones reach parity — no risky full rewrite required.
AI Agent Platform — event-driven, streaming, LLM-nativeBuild now · Ships independently
Operational Knowledge Graph — context-optimized data layerBuild now · Reads from existing APIs
Five Star Rating Engine — real-time scoring serviceBuild now · New revenue surface
API Integration Bridge — translates legacy models for modern servicesTemporary · Replace over 18 months
Legacy Platform — inspections, tasks, training, assetsMaintain · Deprecate gradually

Enterprise Customization — Three Tiers

The deeper a customer customizes SafetyCulture to their specific operations and language, the more it becomes their system. This is what defeats vertical startups — they can have vertical depth, but not your company's depth.

SMB — Self-Serve
Vertical template packs and pre-built agents out of the box. Simple prompt interface for agent customization — no technical knowledge required. "Always escalate any chemical spill to the area manager within 5 minutes."
Mid-Market — Guided Build
Field Deployment Engineer maps company-specific SOPs, org structures, and risk thresholds into the platform. Custom agent workflows via a no-code builder. Company-specific Five Star Rating weightings configured to match internal risk appetite.
Enterprise — Full Custom
Full Agent SDK access. Build proprietary agents on SafetyCulture's data infrastructure. Fine-tune models on company-specific operational data. Private benchmarking within corporate groups. White-label for supply chain partners.

The Five Star Rating is the answer made legible. The agent platform is the answer made actionable. And the data — 10 years, 1 billion checks, 76,000 organizations — is the answer made defensible.

The 5 Strategic Bets

01
Make the Five Star Rating the Industry Standard
Invest in making the Five Star Rating the universal language for operational health — spanning safety, quality, compliance, and asset health for operational health. Partner with major insurers, industry bodies, and regulators to pilot rating-based underwriting and procurement requirements. This turns SafetyCulture's data into a global standard — impossible to displace once embedded.
→ New revenue: rating-as-a-service, API licensing to insurers
02
Build Agent Infrastructure, Not Just Features
Create a dedicated Agent Platform as a product layer — with its own SDK, marketplace, and developer ecosystem. Let customers, FDEs, and partners build on top. Goal: SafetyCulture becomes the Salesforce of operational AI. Platform revenue replaces seat revenue. Network effects replace sales motion.
→ Platform economics: rev-share on marketplace agents, usage pricing
03
Verticalize Deeply, Unify Strategically
Launch distinct vertical products with vertical-specific agents, benchmarks, and UI — each a credible competitor to vertical-only startups. But unify at the platform layer: shared data infrastructure, shared Five Star Rating framework, shared agent SDK. Vertical depth, horizontal scale.
→ Compete with vertical startups while expanding total addressable market
04
Execute the Strangler Fig Migration
Commit to building new around old — not rewriting everything at once. New Agent Platform and Knowledge Graph ship as modern, independent services. Legacy platform maintained but not extended. Year 1 — agent platform in pilot. Year 2 — default for all new customers. Year 3 — migration path offered to legacy base.
→ Innovation velocity without betting the existing business
05
Own the Physical-World Data Moat
Aggressively expand data ingestion: IoT partnerships, wearable integrations, CMMS connectors, and sensor APIs. Define and publish an "Operational Data Standard" — evangelize it, partner on it, become the default format. No competitor has 10 years of this data at this scale, and that gap widens with every customer added.
→ Compounding defensibility — more data → better agents → more customers → more data
